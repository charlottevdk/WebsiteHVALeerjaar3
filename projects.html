The Challenge

Stryker OI needed to execute its participation in the LVO Congress 2026 in Gooiland Hilversum — with half the booth space compared to the previous year (6m²) and a strict compliance process to navigate. The challenge was to maximise commercial impact within tighter constraints.

What I Did

  • Managed full compliance approval process via Cvent and internal review — first time doing this independently
  • Coordinated logistics with sales reps for product selection, choosing strategically which products to display given the reduced space (Neptune® 3, CD NXT, instrument sets)
  • Designed and built an MS Forms lead generation tool, capturing name, hospital, product interest and opt-in consent
  • Managed stakeholder communication across 7+ people: events planner, sales reps, marketing manager, compliance team and congress organisation
  • Attended the congress and represented Stryker at the booth (26–27 March 2026)

Key Decision

I proactively questioned whether to include the SmokeBuster in the product display — it was already being showcased at multiple other Belgian congresses. By raising this, I helped the team make a commercially conscious choice about product placement within limited booth space.

Q1 2026
Market Intelligence — MIS Foot & Orthognathic
Market Research MedTech Strategy Data Visualisation
CE-Skills
KD — Critical Thinking CB — Commercial Awareness CO — Communication

The Challenge

The Stryker OI sales team lacked structured market intelligence on two growing product segments: Minimally Invasive Surgery (MIS) Foot and Orthognathic instruments. They needed data to hold more credible conversations with surgeons — but the data was scattered, complex, and hard to visualise.

What I Did

  • Conducted desk research on procedure volumes for maxillofacial and MIS foot surgery in the Benelux using medical publications, journals and industry databases
  • Analysed Stryker’s portfolio gap: strong in Large/Medium Bone, underserved in Small Bone (maxillofacial)
  • Built a competitive landscape for MIS Foot — identifying Stryker’s position as a ‘fast follower’ with a 12-month window to establish KOL relationships
  • Analysed surgeon learning curves and adoption barriers to inform the sales team’s conversation approach
  • Delivered a full MIS Foot Market Intelligence Report and an educational one-pager for sales reps

Critical Insight

The most valuable output wasn’t the data — it was the interpretation. I challenged myself to go beyond “here is the trend” to “here is what Stryker should do about it, and by when.” The report ends with a prioritised action plan including KOL engagement, structured training, and portfolio gap escalation.

All figures in the deliverable are dummy data in compliance with Stryker’s confidentiality policy. The analytical structure and strategic reasoning are fully representative of the actual research conducted.

— Internship Briefing Contract, Stryker OI Northern Europe, February 2026

Jetpak Warehouse Audit & Inventory System
Operations Stakeholder Management Problem Solving
CE-Skills
KD — Critical Thinking AV — Adaptability SW — Collaboration

The Challenge

Stryker’s marketing materials were stored at an external logistics partner — Jetpak in Mechelen. Sales reps kept reporting incomplete shipments, but no one had a clear picture of why. My task: visit the warehouse, validate the inventory, and build a kit-based system.

What I Did

  • Coordinated and planned the warehouse visit with stakeholders across Belgium, Netherlands and Finland
  • Designed a structured kit-based inventory approach before arrival: kit IDs, colour coding, QR codes linking to an MS Forms reporting tool
  • Conducted the on-site audit (12 May, Mechelen) together with Nazli Tiryaki and Hande Cankaya Keles
  • Discovered the root problem went far deeper than missing parts — fundamental lack of traceability, governance, and stock control
  • Wrote a comprehensive findings report sent to 8+ stakeholders, clearly communicating what was found and what it meant

Adaptability in Action

When the reality on the ground didn’t match the plan, I didn’t pretend otherwise. I adjusted the scope transparently, communicated the deeper root cause, and reframed the audit findings as the essential starting point for a structural solution.

“Thank you once more for your visit to Jetpak and all the great observations you made. As already discussed, this is not the ideal way of storing and managing our equipment and another solution must be found.”

— Juha Soininen, Marketing Manager Northern Europe, 19 May 2026

“I applaud the initiative, this is well needed!”

— Alexis Michaud, Senior Product Manager Trauma BeNeLux, 7 May 2026

The Challenge

Stryker provided Microsoft Copilot to its employees — but few were using it. The tool was available in Outlook, Teams, Word, PowerPoint and Excel, but most colleagues hadn’t explored it beyond the basics. I saw an opportunity to make a practical difference.

What I Did

  • Designed ‘The 15-Minute Gift’ — a focused demo session built around four real-world use cases specific to the Stryker marketing team
  • Created a Stryker-specific Copilot Cheat Sheet with exact prompts for: email thread analysis, follow-up tracking, Teams meeting recaps and Excel data analysis
  • Presented live to the Northern Europe team via MS Teams on 17 April 2026 — attendees from NL, BE, FI, NO and SE
  • Emphasised data security: Copilot (Stryker-approved) vs. external AI tools
  • Opened myself up as a resource: “Don’t hesitate to send me a message if you run into anything”

“Using Copilot and learning to use it in our benefit — I’d like you to share those learnings with the rest of the team, so that everybody can start thinking and testing. We need to work smarter, not harder.”

— Juha Soininen, Marketing Manager Northern Europe, closing the Copilot session, 17 April 2026
