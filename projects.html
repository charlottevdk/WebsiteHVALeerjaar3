The LVO (Landelijke Vereniging van Orthopedisch Instrumentmakers) Congress 2026 was one of the most important commercial touchpoints for Stryker OI in the Dutch market. I took full ownership — from strategic planning and stakeholder alignment through to day-of execution and post-event ROI analysis.

Before LVO, I conducted a landscape analysis of five relevant congresses (LVO, VVOV, Afiso, NOV, BVOT/OB) to evaluate audience profile and commercial relevance. For the event itself, I designed a lead-generation system using Microsoft Forms and coordinated seven stakeholders across logistics, venue, and suppliers. Post-event, I built an ROI analysis framework to measure effectiveness and give Stryker a repeatable method for future event decisions.

Key outcomes
  • Structured lead-capture system used throughout both congress days
  • 7-stakeholder logistics coordinated without delays on either day
  • Post-event ROI report enabling data-driven congress prioritisation
  • 5-congress landscape analysis to inform future events strategy

A comprehensive market intelligence report covering two specialised surgical segments — MIS Foot and Orthognathic surgery — both high-growth areas in the Benelux. Stryker needed a clear picture to inform its go-to-market strategy.

The six-chapter report moves from Benelux procedure volumes through competitive landscape mapping, a surgeon learning curve analysis, and a portfolio gap assessment. The gap analysis revealed Stryker's Large and Medium Bone portfolio is strong, while Small Bone instruments are significantly underutilised relative to market potential — a strategic insight with direct commercial implications.

In parallel, I created an Orthognathic educational guide for sales reps — translating complex surgical context into language that enables more credible customer conversations.

Key outcomes
  • 6-chapter MIS Foot report covering Benelux volumes and competitive positioning
  • Small Bone portfolio gap identified — commercially significant for product strategy
  • Orthognathic educational guide produced to upskill the sales team
  • Learning curve analysis supporting surgeon adoption timelines

In May 2026, I travelled to Mechelen, Belgium with colleagues Nazli Tiryaki and Hande Cankaya Keles to conduct a physical audit of Stryker's marketing asset inventory at the Jetpak warehouse. The objective: locate 26 branded exhibition banners registered as in storage.

We found only 6 of 26. The remaining 20 were unaccounted for — no record of shipping, return, or disposal. I led the root cause analysis, identifying three structural failures: no traceability system, outdated inventory logs, and no governance owner for asset tracking.

The audit findings report recommended a check-out/check-in system, assigned asset ownership, and a regular reconciliation cadence — directly quantifying a financial risk Stryker hadn't previously measured.

Key outcomes
  • Full physical inventory audit — 6 of 26 banners located
  • Three structural root causes identified and documented
  • Findings report with actionable process improvement recommendations
  • Financial risk to marketing operations quantified for the first time

Stryker Cheat Sheet

This project started without a brief. After exploring Microsoft Copilot independently, I saw real potential to save time for the Stryker OI team — built around a simple concept: what if every rep could reclaim 15 minutes per day?

I developed four concrete use cases tailored to the commercial team's workflow — meeting summarisation, email drafting, pre-call briefings, competitive insight synthesis — each with a worked example and estimated time saving. I then presented live to a 5-country team, driving immediate adoption from colleagues who had never used AI tools before.

To sustain adoption, I created a Stryker-specific Copilot Cheat Sheet — a quick-reference card with the most useful prompts, written in Stryker language and context.

Key outcomes
  • 4 use cases with worked examples and time-saving estimates
  • Live demo to 5-country team with immediate independent adoption
  • Stryker-branded Cheat Sheet as a lasting resource
  • AI adoption initiative started independently — not assigned
