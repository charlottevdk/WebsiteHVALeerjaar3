The LVO (Landelijke Vereniging van Orthopedisch Instrumentmakers) Congress 2026 was one of the most important commercial touchpoints for Stryker OI in the Dutch market. I took full ownership of our presence — from strategic planning and stakeholder alignment through to day-of execution and post-event ROI analysis.

Before LVO, I conducted a landscape analysis of five relevant congresses: LVO, VVOV, Afiso, NOV, and BVOT/OB — evaluating each on audience profile, commercial relevance, and Stryker's historical return. This analysis formed the basis for prioritising which events deserved investment in the upcoming calendar year.

For LVO itself, I designed a lead-generation system using Microsoft Forms, enabling the sales team to capture qualified contacts directly on the stand in a structured, trackable way. I coordinated logistics across seven stakeholders — internal colleagues, the venue, and third-party suppliers — and managed the full 6 m² stand setup and breakdown.

Post-event, I built an ROI analysis framework to measure the effectiveness of our attendance against cost and leads generated, giving Stryker a repeatable method for future congress evaluation decisions.

Market Intelligence — MIS Foot & Orthognathic

One of my most analytically demanding projects: a comprehensive market intelligence report covering two specialised surgical segments — MIS Foot (Minimally Invasive Surgery) and Orthognathic surgery (corrective jaw surgery). Both are high-growth areas in the Benelux, and Stryker needed a clear picture to inform its go-to-market strategy.

The report spans six chapters: a macro overview of Benelux procedure volumes, a competitor landscape mapping, a learning curve analysis for surgeons adopting MIS techniques, and a portfolio gap assessment. The gap analysis revealed that Stryker's Large and Medium Bone portfolio is strong, while Small Bone instruments are significantly underutilised relative to market potential — a strategic insight with direct commercial implications.

In parallel, I developed a standalone educational document on Orthognathic surgery for sales representatives — translating complex surgical context into language that helps reps hold credible conversations with surgeons and theatre staff.

The research required synthesising data from clinical publications, procedure volume databases, competitive catalogues, and internal sales data — then translating complex findings into actionable strategic recommendations for the commercial team.

Key outcomes
  • Delivered a 6-chapter MIS Foot market intelligence report covering Benelux volumes and competitive positioning
  • Identified Small Bone portfolio gap — a commercially significant finding for product strategy
  • Produced a separate Orthognathic educational guide to upskill the sales team on clinical context
  • Provided learning curve analysis to support surgeon adoption timelines for MIS techniques

Jetpak Warehouse Audit — Asset Traceability

Physical Audit · Root Cause Analysis · Process Recommendations
6/26

3

3

In May 2026, I travelled to Mechelen, Belgium with colleagues Nazli Tiryaki and Hande Cankaya Keles to conduct a physical audit of Stryker's marketing asset inventory at the Jetpak warehouse. The objective: locate and account for 26 branded exhibition banners registered as being in storage.

We found only 6 of the 26 banners. The remaining 20 were unaccounted for — no record of where they were shipped, returned, or disposed of. This wasn't just a stock discrepancy; it exposed a fundamental gap in asset governance.

I led the root cause analysis, identifying three structural failures: no traceability system (assets moved without check-out records), outdated inventory logs not reconciled with physical counts, and the absence of a governance framework with no one owning responsibility for asset tracking.

The audit findings were compiled into a report with concrete recommendations: implementing a check-out/check-in system, assigning asset ownership per region, and establishing a regular reconciliation cadence. The work directly quantified an operational and financial risk Stryker hadn't previously measured.

Key outcomes
  • Conducted full physical inventory audit — located 6 of 26 registered banners
  • Identified three structural root causes: no traceability, outdated records, no governance owner
  • Delivered a findings report with actionable process improvement recommendations
  • Quantified a financial risk to the marketing operations function for the first time

This project started as a personal initiative. After exploring Microsoft Copilot independently, I saw real potential to save time for the Stryker OI team — specifically the repetitive administrative tasks that eat into selling time. I built a case around a simple concept: what if every rep could reclaim 15 minutes per day?

I developed four concrete Copilot use cases tailored to the commercial team's daily workflow — meeting summarisation, email drafting, pre-call briefings, and competitive insight synthesis. Each use case included a worked example and an estimated time saving per week.

I then presented this live to the team across five countries, demonstrating how Copilot integrates into their existing tools without requiring technical expertise. The response was immediate: colleagues who had never used AI tools started experimenting the same week.

To sustain adoption beyond the presentation, I created a Stryker-specific Copilot Cheat Sheet — a quick-reference card with the most useful prompts and workflows, written in Stryker language and context. It became the team's go-to starting point for AI-assisted work.

Key outcomes
  • Developed 4 Copilot use cases with worked examples and time-saving estimates
  • Delivered live demo to a 5-country team, driving immediate independent adoption
  • Created a Stryker-branded Copilot Cheat Sheet as a lasting team resource
  • Initiated an AI adoption programme independently — without being asked

The OI Academy 201 is Stryker's internal certification programme for commercial team members in the Orthopaedic Instruments division. It is an intensive four-day training covering surgical procedures, instrument systems, clinical environments, and product application — knowledge normally the domain of trained medical sales professionals.

As a marketing intern, I was the only participant without a pure sales or clinical background. Absorbing surgical anatomy, technique steps, and instrument specifications at a pace designed for experienced reps was genuinely outside my comfort zone.

I passed with a score of 80% — exactly meeting the certification threshold. The course average was 85%, placing me within range of my technically experienced peers. More importantly, this training gave me a foundational understanding of the clinical world Stryker operates in — enabling more credible market intelligence, more informed conversations with the sales team, and a clearer sense of what matters at the point of care.

The experience demonstrated adaptability and a commitment to the role beyond its formal scope — showing I was willing to do the hard work of understanding the business from the inside out.
