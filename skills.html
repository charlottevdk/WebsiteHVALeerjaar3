HvA Commercial Economics · Competency Framework

Five competencies.
Proven in practice.

The CE programme develops five core professional skills. Here's how I demonstrated each one through real work at Stryker — not theory, but evidence from projects I owned.

Critical Thinking Teamwork Commercial Awareness Adaptability Communication
Project 02 — Market Intelligence
From “what” to “why” and “what's next”
The MIS Foot report was a structured argument, not a data dump. I moved from procedure volume data through competitive positioning to a portfolio gap assessment that identified Small Bone as Stryker's most underutilised segment — requiring me to cross-reference sources and formulate a strategic recommendation.
Project 03 — Jetpak Audit
Root cause analysis under ambiguity
When only 6 of 26 banners were found, the easy answer was “assets are missing.” I asked: why does this happen systematically? Identifying three structural root causes — and building recommendations from them — demonstrated thinking structurally rather than reactively.
Project 01 — LVO Congress
7-stakeholder coordination
I was the central point of contact for seven internal and external stakeholders across the LVO Congress — drafting communications, chasing confirmations, resolving scheduling conflicts. No delays on either day. Feedback from colleagues reflected a smooth, professional execution they could rely on.
Projects 03 & 05 — Jetpak & OI Academy
Field teamwork and peer collaboration
At Jetpak I worked in a structured 3-person audit team, distributing tasks and synthesising findings together. At OI Academy I participated alongside sales reps with years more experience — contributing to group exercises and building relationships across countries.
Project 01 — LVO Congress
ROI-first event strategy
Rather than treating LVO as a logistics exercise, I built an ROI analysis framework connecting event cost to leads generated — and a 5-congress landscape analysis to prioritise which events deserve investment based on commercial return. Marketing spend as a commercial decision.
Project 02 — Market Intelligence
Portfolio gap as revenue signal
Identifying Small Bone underutilisation wasn't a product catalogue observation — it was a commercial signal pointing to a potential revenue gap where Stryker has capability but insufficient market penetration. Translating market data to revenue implication.
Project 04 — Copilot AI
Self-directed adoption of new technology
The Copilot initiative started without a brief. I identified the tool, taught myself to use it, built use cases from scratch, and presented to five countries within weeks. Adaptability not as reaction, but as a deliberate choice to get ahead of change.
Project 05 — OI Academy 201
Succeeding outside my background
As the only non-clinical participant in OI Academy 201, I had to adapt my learning approach to absorb surgical anatomy at a pace designed for experienced reps. Passed with 80%. The experience fundamentally changed how I understand Stryker's commercial context.
Project 04 — Copilot AI
Cross-country live presentation
Presenting to a 5-country mixed audience required adapting for tech-comfortable and sceptical participants alike. I structured the message around the “15-minute gift” — a concrete, tangible outcome. The Cheat Sheet added a second communication challenge: distilling complex capability into a one-page reference for a busy commercial audience.
Projects 01 & 03 — LVO & Jetpak
Written communication in high-stakes contexts
LVO stakeholder communications needed to be clear and action-oriented across seven parties. The Jetpak findings report had to flag sensitive operational gaps without creating blame — framing, evidence, and recommendation sequenced to build trust rather than defensiveness.
