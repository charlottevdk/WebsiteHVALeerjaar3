KD
Critical Thinking
Kritisch Denken
Development level
Par. 2.1.1 — Market Intelligence Par. 2.4.1 — Jetpak Audit

How I understand this skill

Critical thinking means more than reading sources and summarising them. It means questioning assumptions, connecting dots, and producing conclusions that are actionable — not just accurate. At Stryker, I learned to ask not just “what does the data say?” but “what should we do about it, and by when?”

Evidence 1 — MIS Foot Market Research

My MIS Foot research report didn’t stop at trend analysis. I interrogated why the Benelux was 3–4 years behind France in adoption, analysed the competitive landscape, identified the surgeon learning curve as a key barrier, and translated all of this into a prioritised action plan for the sales team.

Key insight from the report

The competitor with the strongest training programme wins the market — not the one with the best product. Stryker’s response should be an enablement strategy, not a product push.

Evidence 2 — Jetpak Warehouse Audit

When the warehouse visit didn’t deliver what we had planned, I analysed why. Rather than reporting “we couldn’t finish the inventory,” I identified the root causes: no traceability system, no governance structure, outdated records, no return-reporting process. My findings report reframed the audit as providing valuable root cause insight — a more honest and useful output.

SW
Collaboration
Samenwerken
Development level
Par. 2.2.1 — LVO Stakeholders Par. 2.4.1 — Jetpak Team Par. 2.5.2 — OI Academy

How I understand this skill

Effective collaboration isn’t just being easy to work with. It means choosing the right people, communicating clearly, and creating conditions for others to contribute meaningfully. At Stryker I regularly worked across departments, countries, and seniority levels.

Evidence 1 — LVO Congress Stakeholder Coordination

For the LVO Congress I coordinated seven stakeholders simultaneously — events manager, two sales reps, marketing manager, compliance, congress organisation and a colleague on setup. I mapped each person’s priorities, addressed potential conflicts proactively, and kept everyone aligned via a single, structured update email.

Evidence 2 — Jetpak Collaboration & Feedback

I proactively asked Dominique Benoit for permission to bring colleague Hande Cankaya Keles on the warehouse visit — including a fully planned train itinerary. After the visit, Hande gave structured feedback on the kit-system I had built, treating it as a serious piece of work.

“I think the kit-based structure is a really solid starting point. Grouping the materials this way already brings much more clarity compared to tracking individual items.”

— Hande Cankaya Keles, Marketing Stryker Instruments Europe, 8 May 2026
CB
Commercial Awareness
Commercieel Bewustzijn
Development level
Par. 2.1.1 — Market Intelligence Par. 2.3.1 — LVO ROI Par. 2.5.1 — Representing Stryker

How I understand this skill

Commercial awareness means understanding how business decisions translate into results — and making those connections explicit. It also means being able to identify where opportunities are being left on the table.

Evidence 1 — LVO Lead Generation

I built the MS Forms lead capture system specifically to make the event’s ROI measurable. Every field was designed with the sales follow-up process in mind: hospital name, product interest, opt-in consent. This transformed the congress from a branding exercise into a pipeline-generating activity.

Evidence 2 — MIS Foot Opportunity Identification

The MIS Foot research identified a specific commercial window: Stryker is approximately 12–18 months behind Competitor A in the Benelux, but the market hasn’t consolidated yet. I translated this into a timed action plan — KOL engagement now, structured training by Q3 — to position Stryker before it’s too late.

AV
Adaptability
Aanpassingsvermogen
Development level
Par. 2.3.2 — Copilot Demo Par. 2.4.1 — Jetpak Audit Par. 2.5.2 — OI Academy

How I understand this skill

Adaptability isn’t just reacting to change — it’s staying effective when the plan changes, learning new domains quickly, and adjusting communication style to the situation and audience.

Evidence 1 — OI Academy 201

Learning surgical power tool technology as a marketing intern — alongside sales reps who work with this equipment daily — required rapid adaptation to a highly technical domain completely outside my training. Within four days I passed the formal assessment and was able to hold credible product conversations at the LVO Congress.

Evidence 2 — Copilot Demo: audience adaptation

I presented AI tools to a senior, multi-national audience that ranged from tech-savvy to tech-averse. I consciously adapted my language, examples, and pace — avoiding technical jargon and focusing entirely on what saves time in their specific daily workflows.

Evidence 3 — Jetpak: adjusting scope under pressure

When the warehouse visit couldn’t deliver the planned output, I didn’t pretend otherwise. I adjusted the scope in real-time, documented what we could, and reframed the findings as something more valuable — root cause analysis — rather than a failed task.

CO
Communication
Communicatie
Development level
Par. 2.2.1 — LVO Stakeholders Par. 2.3.1 — Event Reporting Par. 2.3.2 — Copilot Demo Par. 2.5.1 — Representing Stryker

How I understand this skill

Communication at Stryker means being understood by very different audiences: a surgeon at a congress stand, a marketing manager in a Teams meeting, a logistics partner in Mechelen, and a compliance officer reviewing an event request. The content is the same — the form has to change.

Evidence 1 — Copilot Demo

Presenting AI tools to a multi-national senior team is a communication challenge as much as a technical one. I designed ‘The 15-Minute Gift’ to be entirely audience-centric — every use case mapped to a real pain point the team had voiced before. The session ended with spontaneous positive reactions from participants in five countries.

Evidence 2 — Jetpak Findings Report

Communicating disappointing findings to management is a delicate communication task. My 13 May email was structured, factual, and constructive — it acknowledged the scope limitation, explained the root causes clearly, and ended with an open invitation to align on next steps. Dominique Benoit forwarded it directly to senior management as a useful analysis.

“Dank je wel voor het initiatief en het duidelijke plan van aanpak.”

— Kirsty Thomass, Sales Representative, 11 May 2026
