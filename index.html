About me

Who I am as a professional

I’m a third-year Commercial Economics (Global Marketing) student at the Hogeschool van Amsterdam, where I graduated as valedictorian with a GPA of 8.2. Before that, I was also valedictorian in Fashion Design — a detail that says a lot about how seriously I take ownership of my work.

My internship at Stryker Orthopaedic Instruments Northern Europe (Feb–Jun 2026) placed me at the intersection of marketing strategy, market intelligence and operational excellence. From organizing the LVO Congress in Hilversum to auditing a logistics warehouse in Mechelen and introducing Microsoft Copilot to a pan-European team — I thrive when I combine analytical thinking with clear, decisive communication.

Marketing, for me, is the perfect blend of psychology, creativity and strategy. I believe the best marketing doesn’t sell features — it tells stories that move people. I learned that first-hand at CHANEL, where shifting from product specs to emotional storytelling helped my team exceed a €100,000 weekly sales target, repeatedly.
