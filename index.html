About

Who I am

I'm a final-year Commercial Economics student at the Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences, completing my internship at Stryker Orthopaedic Instruments Northern Europe. I graduate as valedictorian with a GPA of 8.2.

My work sits at the intersection of analytical thinking and communication. I enjoy projects where I synthesise complex information into commercially relevant decisions — market intelligence, events strategy, AI adoption, operational audits.

I want to build a career in MedTech or a similarly complex B2B sector, where rigour and purpose reinforce each other.

  • I move from data to insight to recommendation — not stopping at description. Both the MIS Foot intelligence report and the Jetpak audit required getting past the surface to identify what was structurally wrong.
  • I adapt message to audience — from a 5-country live demo, to a careful written findings report that needed to flag sensitive gaps without creating defensiveness.
  • I connect marketing activity to business outcomes. The LVO ROI framework and the Small Bone portfolio gap finding both came from asking the commercial question behind the data.
  • The Copilot project wasn't assigned — I identified the opportunity, built it from scratch, and presented it to five countries. I work best when trusted to determine the approach.
  • A 5-country Northern Europe team at Stryker gave me real exposure to different communication styles, decision-making paces, and professional cultures.
