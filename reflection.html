Young
Professional

Six months at Stryker OI Northern Europe taught me a lot — about medical technology, about international business, and about myself as a professional. This is my honest reflection on where I've been, what I've learned, and where I want to go.

Industry fit

Does the MedTech sector suit me?

✓ Yes — with conviction

Medical technology was an entirely unfamiliar world for me before this internship. Surgical power tools, operating room protocols, compliance regulations, Key Opinion Leader strategies — none of this was part of my vocabulary six months ago.

What surprised me most was how commercially rigorous the sector is. Every decision — which product to display at a congress, which surgeon to target first, which market to enter — is grounded in data, competitive analysis, and a clear link to patient outcomes. That combination of analytical depth and real-world impact is exactly what I want from a career.

I also discovered a genuine interest in the intersection of healthcare and technology. The MIS Foot research project made me realise how much innovation is happening in surgical technique — and how much marketing can do to accelerate adoption and, ultimately, improve patient care.

Work type fit

Does this type of work suit me?

✓ Yes — especially the strategic and communicative parts

My work at Stryker spanned four very different types of activity: event management, market research, operational problem-solving, and knowledge sharing. I learned a lot about where I get energy and where I don't.

Where I thrived

  • Projects with full ownership and clear business impact
  • Combining analysis with communication (Jetpak report, MIS Foot research)
  • Working with diverse stakeholders across different levels
  • Creating structure where there was none

Where I grew the most

  • Presenting to senior international audiences
  • Adapting scope when reality didn't match the plan
  • Technical product knowledge (outside comfort zone)
  • Navigating complex multi-party compliance processes

I noticed that I lose energy on purely administrative tasks — updating spreadsheets, tracking fields in Cvent. But give me a problem that needs structure, a clear analytical framework, and a stakeholder who needs to understand the outcome, and I'm in my element.

Company type fit

Does a multinational like Stryker suit me?

✓ Yes — the scale and ambition energise me

Stryker is a global company with a high-performance culture. Results matter, standards are high, and the pace is fast. For some people, that's intimidating. For me, it was motivating.

What I valued most was being treated as a genuine contributor from day one. Juha included me in management-level email threads, gave me ownership of real deliverables, and asked for my input in strategic discussions. That level of trust accelerated my development significantly.

The international dimension was also formative. Working alongside colleagues from Finland, Belgium, Norway, Sweden and the Netherlands — and presenting to all of them in one Teams session — gave me a much clearer sense of what international collaboration actually demands: cultural awareness, clear written communication, and the confidence to take up space in a room even when you're the youngest person in it.

Culture fit

Does the Stryker culture suit me?

✓ Strong fit — especially the autonomy and ambition

Stryker's culture is results-driven and ambitious, but also genuinely collegial. My manager was supportive and direct — he gave feedback clearly, celebrated wins, and pushed me to keep raising the bar. Colleagues across the team were welcoming and responsive, even when I was asking questions that added to their workload.

I particularly valued the trust culture: nobody micromanaged me. I was expected to plan my own projects, meet my own deadlines, and come to 1:1s with an agenda. That kind of autonomy brought out the best in me.

Development plan

What do I still want to learn?

  • 01
    Data Analytics — Power BI & SQL I relied on Excel and manual analysis throughout this internship. I want to build the technical skills to create interactive dashboards and handle larger datasets independently — so I'm not bottlenecked by data volume or format.
  • 02
    Executive Presentation Skills The Copilot demo went well, but I noticed I could project more authority in front of senior audiences. I want to intentionally practise high-stakes presentation scenarios during the rest of my studies.
  • 03
    Cross-cultural Communication Working across five countries gave me a foundation. But I want to understand more deeply how cultural context shapes communication styles — particularly in negotiations and conflict resolution.
  • 04
    MedTech Regulatory Framework CE marking, MDR compliance, clinical evidence requirements — these are the rules of the game in medical technology. For a future career in this space, I need a stronger foundation here.
Looking forward

What kind of professional do I want to be?

This internship gave me a much clearer picture of where I want to land. I want to work in an environment that is international, data-driven, and commercially ambitious — ideally in medical technology, life sciences, or an adjacent sector where work has a genuine impact on people's lives.

Ideal company

International, data-driven culture. Scale of a multinational with room to own real projects. MedTech, life sciences or health-adjacent.

Ideal role

Strategic marketing, market intelligence or commercial excellence. Where analysis meets communication and decisions matter.

What I bring

Structured thinking, strong communication, project ownership, adaptability, and genuine curiosity about the markets I work in.

"We need to work smarter, not harder. And if we have these tools and Stryker is providing them for us, why not use them for our benefit?"
— Juha Soininen, Marketing Manager Northern Europe — closing my Copilot training session, April 2026
